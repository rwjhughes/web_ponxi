words from Hearing the Cloud by Emile Frankel



Shared online listening converts
'this sound changed me',
to 'this sound changed us,'
and this is an equally
beautiful and terrifying thing.

Can fiction influ-
ence future politics?

Can abstract expression be a
form of concrete story-telling?

Does it matter if your artistic
dystopia is ironic or sincere?

Can ironic dystopias bring
about actual dystopias?

Is it that speaking badly
is a form of protection?

Is dystopian art a
reflection and a warning?

What happens when we
'speak badly' of the future?

Music without words functions as an alembic,
as something that distils the vibe
of a wider culture and politics
into a sonic material
functioning in part as representation,
and in part as an actual
embodiment of these condensed tones.

Does the future causally
come into being through
the act of talking about it?

Can music be a curse?
